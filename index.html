Kungawo Prince Manjo

Kungawo Othandwayo "Prince" Manjo (born 11 December 2012), professionally known as Mtholisi, Jolisi, Mfoka Mphill, and Kay, is a South African recording artist, songwriter, vocalist, Maskandi musician, and multi-genre music producer from the Western Cape, South Africa. He is recognized as an emerging independent artist whose work combines traditional South African storytelling with modern music production, allowing him to create music that reflects both cultural heritage and contemporary influences.

Early Life and Education

Born and raised in the Western Cape, Manjo developed a passion for music, creativity, and storytelling from an early age. He attended Eluxolweni Public Primary School, where he first began exploring artistic expression and songwriting. He later enrolled at Siphamandla Senior Secondary School, where he continued developing both his academic and creative abilities. Alongside music, he has expressed a strong interest in engineering and technology, which remain among his future ambitions.

Musical Career

Manjo's journey into music began through independent experimentation with songwriting, recording, and digital production. Using FL Studio Mobile, he taught himself music production, beat-making, vocal arrangement, recording techniques, and song composition. His dedication to learning production enabled him to develop his own sound while gaining experience across multiple genres.

Before pursuing a solo career, he performed as part of the music duo Kay & Mekeni CPT, where he worked alongside Ovayo Mekeni. The partnership provided valuable experience in collaboration, songwriting, and independent music distribution.

In 2025, he officially entered the music industry with the release of his debut single, Ushaya Abafana. Although the release achieved modest recognition, it marked the beginning of his professional career as a recording artist.

In 2026, he released Uzofa Remix, which gained significant online attention and accumulated more than 11,000 views on YouTube. The release became one of his most recognized projects and helped expand his audience beyond his local community.

As a producer, Manjo has explored a variety of South African musical styles, including Maskandi, Gqom, Afro-Pop, and other contemporary genres. His versatility has established him as a multi-genre producer capable of adapting traditional influences to modern audiences.

Artistic Identities

Throughout his career, Manjo has worked under several artistic identities. The name Mtholisi represents his Maskandi music career, while Jolisi reflects a creative identity inspired by the acclaimed South African artist Mjolisi. He is also known as Mfoka Mphill and Kay, the latter being associated with his earlier collaborative projects and independent productions.

Influences

Among the most significant influences on his musical development is renowned South African Maskandi musician Mjolisi. Inspired by Mjolisi's storytelling ability, authenticity, and production style, Manjo developed a deep appreciation for traditional Maskandi music while working to establish a distinctive artistic voice of his own.

He has also credited his late grandfather, Phillip Tunapayi Manjo, as one of the greatest inspirations in his life and career. The encouragement and legacy of his grandfather continue to motivate his artistic journey. Additionally, he acknowledges the support and guidance of his grandmother, Zodwa Manjo, whose presence has played an important role throughout his personal and creative development.

Personal Life

Manjo is the son of Zingiswa Asonge Tafile and Mhlangabezi Tholakele Tafile. Outside music, he remains focused on his education while continuing to build his artistic career. He aims to contribute positively to South African music through cultural storytelling, creativity, and innovation.

Legacy and Future Aspirations

Despite his young age, Kungawo Prince Manjo continues to establish himself as a promising independent musician, songwriter, vocalist, and producer. His long-term vision includes expanding his catalogue, reaching wider audiences, pursuing engineering, and creating music that preserves cultural heritage while embracing modern production techniques.
